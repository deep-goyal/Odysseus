Data Structures and Algorithms

We are here. This is going to be a life defining moment for you. Now that you are comfortable with a programming language, in this section, you will learn how to manipulate data and perform tasks to solve problems, the real life kind. Your first task here is to get a general understanding of this section. Thankfully, Tren has published a stunning video↗ to help you out here.

Your strategy here is simple. You have to learn data structures and algorithms by referring to Steven Skiena's Algorithm Design Manual↗ and solving a whole lot of LeetCode↗ problems.

Dr. Skiena proves to have an acute interest and a unique way to approach a problem logically in his book. If you ever feel the need for other resources, here are my recommendations -- NeetCode's Youtube Lectures↗ and Tim RoughGarden's Lectures↗

ChatGPT↗ might be helpful in learning new data structures and finding the code syntax instantly too. I would recommend using whatever resources you can to solve the problems, the aim here is to understand how to logically break down a problem into smaller chunks and dealing with each of them algorithmically.

If you are done, congratulations! We are moving to an exciting part of this learning track--Web Development.
